Non-Combustible Cladding: Why It Matters

Following the Grenfell Tower fire in London (2017) and similar incidents worldwide, building codes across the UK, EU, Middle East, and Asia have mandated non-combustible cladding for high-rise buildings, public venues, hospitals, schools, and other critical structures.

A "non-combustible" cladding panel must achieve an A2 classification or higher under EN 13501-1 (A2-s1,d0), or equivalent ratings under local standards. This means the panel material:

  • Does not ignite or contribute to fire spread
  • Produces minimal smoke (s1 rating — critical for evacuation)
  • Produces no flaming droplets (d0 rating — prevents fire spread downward)

The Zero Water Absorption Advantage

Unlike conventional non-combustible panels that use mineral-powder-filled cores (which absorb 3-7% moisture), Hengdu's cladding panels use a screw-extruded solid mineral core with near-zero porosity. This means:

  • No delamination in high-humidity or rain-exposed environments
  • No dimensional changes — the panel stays flat and true over decades
  • No freeze-thaw damage in cold climates
  • No peel strength degradation — bonding remains at full strength
  • Lower maintenance — no panel replacement due to moisture failure

Compliance with International Building Codes

UK Building Regulations (Approved Document B) now require non-combustible (Class A2-s1,d0 or better) cladding on buildings over 18m. Hengdu panels meet BS 476 Class 0 and EN 13501-1 A2-s1,d0.
