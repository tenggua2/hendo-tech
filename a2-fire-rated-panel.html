What Is an A2 Fire Rated Aluminium Composite Panel?

An A2 fire rated aluminium composite panel (ACP) is a building cladding material consisting of two thin aluminium sheets bonded to a mineral-based core. The "A2" classification refers to the highest non-combustible rating under the European standard EN 13501-1, meaning the material does not contribute to fire. A2-s1,d0 specifically means: no smoke production (s1) and no flaming droplets (d0).

Unlike standard ACP panels (which may be B1 or lower rated), A2 panels are required for high-rise buildings, public venues, and projects in jurisdictions with strict fire codes — including the UK (post-Grenfell), the EU, the Middle East, and increasingly across Asia.

Why Hengdu's A2 Panel Is Different: Zero Water Absorption

Most A2 aluminium composite panels on the market use a mineral-powder-filled core produced by hot-pressing. This process creates microscopic pores in the core that absorb moisture — typically 3% to 7% by weight. Over time, water absorption causes:

  • Delamination — the aluminium skins separate from the core
  • Warping and bubbling — visible surface defects on the facade
  • Peel strength loss — up to 20% reduction after moisture exposure
  • Costly callbacks — replacement of failed panels on completed projects

Hengdu Technology solved this problem. Our proprietary screw-extrusion process uses an organic-inorganic supramolecular coupling technology to produce a dense, pore-free A2 core with water absorption below 0.1% — effectively zero. This means our panels will not delaminate, warp, or degrade in any climate condition, from tropical humidity to desert heat.
